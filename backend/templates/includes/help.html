Welcome to Pong!

Pong is a classic arcade game that simulates table tennis. The objective is to score points by making the ball pass your opponent’s paddle.

Getting Started

Objective: Score points by hitting the ball past your opponent's paddle. Each time you score, you earn a point. The player who scores the most points by the end of the game wins.

Game Modes:

  • Two Players: Play against a friend on the same computer.
  • Four Players: Two teams (2 players each) play against each other on the same computer.

Controls

Two Players Controls

Player Paddle (Left Side):

  • Q: Move paddle up.
  • A: Move paddle down.

Player Paddle (Right Side):

  • P: Move paddle up.
  • L: Move paddle down.

Four Players Controls

Player 3 (Left Side):

  • D: Move paddle up.
  • C: Move paddle down.

Player 4 (Right Side):

  • J: Move paddle up.
  • N: Move paddle down.

Scoring System

  • 1 Point: Awarded when the ball passes your opponent’s paddle and hits the edge of the screen.
  • Winning: The game continues until a player reaches the winning score, set before the game starts (e.g., 5 or 11 points), maximum allowed is 11 points.

Troubleshooting

  • Ball Stuck: If the ball gets stuck or doesn’t move, try restarting the game.
  • Controls Not Working: Ensure your keyboard or controller is properly connected. Check in the game settings to see if controls are mapped correctly.

Custom Settings

Adjust Difficulty: For Single Player mode, you can change the computer’s difficulty level in the game settings or customize according to your preferences. There is a special option for visually impaired gamers, with high contrast colors.
